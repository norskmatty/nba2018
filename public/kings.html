Outlook

What a mess of a team. Its top paid player is a 36-year-old seeing out the string. 4.5% of it's cap space will be consumed by a pair of 38-year-olds that retired at least two seasons ago, and the 13th overall pick from the 2016 draft (Georgios Papagiannis) whom they already gave up on. De'Aaron Fox, Willie Cauley-Stein and Buddy Hield essentially make up the 'core' of this team, such as it is.

Oh yes, and they still owe next year's pick to Philadelphia (unprotected, mind you) because they wanted to dump salary to sign Rajon Rondo all the way back in 2015. They have missed the playoffs in each of the past 12 seasons, winning more than 33 games once in that span. They appear completely dysfunctional and, in fact, are.

If Iman Shumpert, Kosta Koufos and Garrett Temple opt-out of their contracts, Sacramento has up to $53 million to throw at whatever desperate player wants their money. As much as those players might want to leave Sacramento, however, the cap space climate doesn't make opting out sound appealing for any of them. And anyway, Sacramento would be better served focusing on their young players Fox, Cauley-Stein and even Hield, who will be joined by at worst the 7th or 8th pick and another first round pick from last year, Justin Jackson.

It's not super enticing, and with no first round pick next year, another bad season won't even earn another shot at another impact player. They can stay open for business to take on salary and see if they can coax any draft capital out of that maneuver. Or, since they have no reason to assure themselves of staying in the lottery, they could indeed some more veterans as one of the few teams with actual cap space to spend.

The Kings shouldn't be too aggressive with that, however. If a young player seeing free agency for the first time is available and actually wants to sign, even if it's as a restricted free agent, then the Kings should be interested. If it's more players on the wrong side of 30, the Kings should lay off.

As for what to look for next season for Kings' fans, the ideal things would be to see growth in De'Aaron Fox's game, anything other than shooting showing up in Buddy Hield's game, a continuation of the defensive prowess that Cauley-Stein is flashing, and that their front office goes at least a few months without humiliating themselves. I'll let you decide which of those is least likely.
