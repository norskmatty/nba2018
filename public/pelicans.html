Outlook

DeMarcus Cousins and Anthony Davis worked, by God. The Pelicans were playing well, added great play from Jrue Holiday, and looked to play a big man foil to the small ball infiltrating the rest of the league. And then Cousins tore his Achilles.

The Pelicans didn't crash, however. They traded for Nikola Mirotic and Anthony Davis went supernova in order to bring them to the playoffs, where they crushed Portland before predictably falling to Golden State in the second round. Now they need to figure out where to go from here.

First off is deciding what to pay Cousins. Big men returning from torn Achilles do not have a great track record of success, and if he isn't good the cap-strapped Pelicans would be in some trouble. There's also the problem that one of Mirotic or Cousins would be unable to play in crunch time, which for this team represents poor resource management. It's a good three-man big rotation the Pelicans could roll out along with Davis, but that would be much less useful come playoff time.

Bringing back Cousins puts them over the cap and risks breaking through the luxury tax line. That's because they need to would then need to decide whether to bring back Rajon Rondo. Rondo was a good fit on the team last season, and might have even played his way into a better contract in spite of his age. Bringing back both Cousins and Rondo almost assures playing the tax, unless someone else is dropped. The issue with not bringing them back is that even without them, the Pelicans would still be over the cap and will not be able to replace them with players of similar quality on the cheap.

The biggest need for the Pelicans is one that is the most difficult to fill: a small forward. Darius Miller can kind of fill it, same with Solomon Hill. But neither is ideal (especially Hill). Without cap space, their options to fill that hole are very limited as they are competing with just about every other team for help there. Getting a quality player there is the key to unlocking this team's true potential, though.

If Cousins comes back and looks good, they could go back to the Davis/Cousins Pelicans and see what they can get for Mirotic. Dangling him and a pick could be what they need to acquire a decent small forward. It's not ideal to keep trading away first round picks (they used one to get Mirotic in the first place) but the Pelicans are working to keep Davis around and happy, and filling the massive hole at the 3 so that they can possibly compete with the best of the West is how they can do that. Will it be enough? It's a small chance, but it's better than no chance.
