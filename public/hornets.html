Outlook

The Charlotte Hornets are hopelessly over the cap and threatening to dip into the luxury tax. That's fine, because it's worth paying a team like this after it won...let me just check my notes here...36 games. Oh. Oh dear. I guess the only positive is that Dwight Howard's contract ends after this season.

That's a good thing, because the top of the list of things Charlotte needs to do is figure out if they want to extend Kemba Walker, also a free agent at the end of this season. Walker is worth keeping much more than Howard, but will also want a significant pay increase. This is a team paying Nicolas Batum, Michael Kidd-Gilchrist, Marvin Williams and Cody Zeller a combined $64.6M next season, and all of them could be back for more in 2019 (MKG and Williams have player options, Zeller and Batum are signed through 2020).

The Hornets (Michael Jordan specifically) went well out of their way to make sure it was clear that they were not interested in trading Walker at last season's trade deadline. That's all well and good, but now they need to either decide if they want to trade him and get something back for him, or extend him. While Walker is very good, the rest of the Hornets are not and with he already at 28, it might make more sense to use him to start a rebuild. Or a build, since there's nothing here really to rebuild.

Otherwise...this team is actually pretty much set for next season already, except it's the same team that just disappointed. Batum is a nasty contract to try to move, useful as he may be to a contender, and Howard is basically a dinosaur at this point. This team is old and expensive and Malik Monk is really the only player to have much hope for the future in.

It's not really clear what the point of keeping Walker around is other than to maybe, MAYBE sneak in as the eighth seed. The Hornets are kind of bad and mostly older, which is a combo that often leads to utter irrelevance. They need some luck and some time to get past their previous roster mistakes, and admitting they need to start over is only the first step in a painful, but necessary, overhaul.
