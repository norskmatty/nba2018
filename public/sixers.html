Outlook

First, let's get this out of the way: Blessed be Sam Hinkie, he who died for this bounty.

Okay. The thing about being ahead of schedule in your process is handling it without going overboard. The Sixers won 17 of their final 18 to end the season (against mostly bad teams, but still) and then took their first playoff series since 2012 4-1 over the Heat. Ben Simmons instantly became a force despite an aversion to shooting so severe he never even considers the idea. Joel Embiid dominated for a full season (well, 63 games before a freak injury ended his regular season early). Robert Covington displayed true 3-and-D skills. Markelle Fultz...well, ok, it wasn't perfect.

After winning 52 games, things are going so well for the Sixers that they might change their name to the Philadelphia Processes. And thanks to the depth of the Process (blessed be Hinkie), Philadelphia has 3 1st round picks the next two seasons and 6 2nd round picks and honestly they don't have enough room to make use of all these picks.

Bringing back T.J. McConnell for basically the minimum is easy. But after that, they have a lot of cap space (if they renounce J.J. Redick and Amir Johnson) and they will definitely be wooing the big boys in free agency now. LeBron is on the table, even if that's unlikely. Paul George is on the table, that's somewhat more likely. Those draft picks, once hoarded so closely, might start going in the opposite direction if the right move can be made. Not only can this team be good, they might need to get in a bit of an arms race with Boston (apologies, Giannis).

Following his bizarre season where he lost his jump shot during the offseason, could they trade Fultz? They couldn't play him in the playoffs, and if he can't shoot he might not be a great pairing with Simmons. Surrounding Simmons with shooters opened up the Sixers offense, but they'll need to decide if they'll bring back Redick and mid-season minimum players Marco Belinelli and Ersan Ilyasova, and for how much. As for Fultz, his value is likely at a nadir, so a trade is unlikely, but who knows? His season was so weird, another weird thing would no longer be surprising.

If the Sixers can't attract LeBron/George, they can try re-signing Redick to another big one-year contract and trying again in 2019. There isn't a lot else out there to break the bank for, though. The Sixers shouldn't be in a rush to fritter away their cap space like some teams might (*cough* Bucks *cough* Pelicans *cough*).

Ideally, Simmons and Fultz go to shooting school this summer and emerge with functional jumpshots and the Sixers rampage over everyone next year. Outside of fantasy land, trust the process, trust internal improvements, and maybe sign Paul George and make this team a monster.
